ACADEMIC POLICIES AND PROCEDURE


NSSC is true to its mission to procedure quality graduates who can compete with the global workforce.
A standard grading system has been adopted for fairness, equality and for effective evaluation of learning among students.

ATTENDANCE
Students shall attend all their classes as scheduled.
Absences from classes must be accounted for by an excused letter signed by their parent(s) or guardian(s) or physician in case of illness.
The letter will be countersigned by the adviser, and should be attached to the attendance slip to be presented to the subject teacher by a class monitor.

In case of three unexcused absences – The parent(s) or guardian(s) will be notified by the adviser and the student will be sent to the Guidance Office.

Cutting of Classes in any subject is equivalent to one half day absence.

A frequently tardy student must present a letter from his/her parent(s) or guardian(s). Three times tardiness is equivalent to one day absence.

Habitual offender or a student who has more than 10% of the total number of school days (4 to 6 Absences per quarter) shall be given final warning and sent to the Guidance Office for Counseling.
Failure to improve shall be dropped from the school (refer to the Attendance Provision and Sanctions, pp. 59)

Note: Students who dropped or are forced dropped from the school must secure clearance and dropping form at the Registrar’s Office.


EXAMINATION
The School Year is divided in Four (4) Grading Periods. Each Grading Period is Divided into Preliminary and Periodical Examinations.

Schedule of the said examination will be posted in the Bulletin Board a week before the Scheduled Examination.

Students are required to pay their monthly dues before securing examination permit at the Cashier’s Office.
Student who failed to pay monthly dues will not be given permit to take exam unless given permission by the school president.

Students who failed to take exams in the scheduled day will not be given special consideration unless his/her reasons have been validated by his/her adviser.
No special exams will be given after grades for the said grading period have been made.

GRADING SYSTEM (Adopted from DepEd Order No. 8, s. 2015)
The K-12 Basic Education Program uses standards — and competency-based grading system. All grades will be summative assessments.
The minimum grade needed to pass a specific learning area is 60, which is transmuted to the report card are 60 for Quarterly Grades and Final Grades.

For these guidelines, the Department will use a floor grade considered as the lowest possible grade that will appear in a learner’s report card.

Learners from Grade 1 to 12 are graded on Written Work, Performance Tasks, and Quarterly Assessment/Exams every quarter.
These three are given specific percentage weights that vary according to the nature of the learning area.

REPORT CARD
Report Cards are given to the parent(s)/guardian(s) during Parents-Teachers Conference that is held after each grading period to clarify matters with the teachers or for consultation.
Tampering the Report Card is considered as serious offense (See Provision and Sanctions, pp.60)

SCHOLASTIC DELINQUENCIES
Elementary and high school students with one (1) or two (2) subjects failing marks shall be re-admitted provided he/she has no delinquency record, took and passed summer class.
Elementary and high school students with/more than three (3) subjects failing marks shall not be re-admitted on the following school year
